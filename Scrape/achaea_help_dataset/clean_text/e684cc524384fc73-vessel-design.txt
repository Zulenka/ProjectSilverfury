9.37.10.2 Vessel Design
See also
HELP CRAFTING GUIDELINES
Having gained the
Beverages
skill
(see
HELP
Beverages for instructions on how to do so) and specialised in Vessels, you are now ready to start designing your
drinking
vessels!
Below is a comprehensive
guide
from start to finish on how to
design
and eventually create a drinking vessel, including any and all relevant rules and guidelines to ensure your design matches Achaea's standard of
writing
and acceptable content.
To quickly get a batch of vessels made:
Use
NDS
LISTPUBLIC V to see all the vessel
designs
that you have access to.
Select one you like, and use NDS P <design#> to examine it in further detail.
Visit the
denizen
specified and REQUEST <nds ID#> [
PASSCODE
] FROM <denizen>
If you do not have enough
gold
in your hands this will inform you of the gold cost.
To design your own vessel, visit the Beverages cellar in
Delos
and purchase the vessel diagram that best matches the type of vessel you want to design.
Then, DESIGN CREATE <diagram> to create your
sketch
in the
system
!
Vessels Syntaxes
-----------------
REQUEST <nds ID#> [PASSCODE] FROM <denizen>
- Have an appropriate denizen create vessels for you.
- For more information on
passcodes
, see
HELP NDS
- This has a gold cost per vessel.
- Jessie the glassblower in New
Thera
will make glass-based vessels.
- Gazban the potter in El'
Jazira
will make clay-based vessels.
- Palusta the stonemason in
Xhaiden
Dale will make stone-based vessels.
- Tobe the woodcarver in Caer Witrin will make
wood
and
bone
-based vessels.
- Rico the blacksmith in New Thera will make metal-based vessels.
- Vanac the leatherworker in
Bitterfork
will make
leather
-based vessels.
DESIGN CREATE <diagram>
- Create a new sketch and
apply
the details of your chosen diagram to it.
- This will assign the
base
name
and type of the item. E.G "stein", or
"snifter".
- This will not consume your diagram.
DESIGN <sketch> COPY <pattern>
- Apply the details of your chosen
pattern
to an existing sketch.
- This will assign the base name and type of the item. E.G "stein", or
"snifter".
- This will not consume your diagram.
GENERAL NOTE: Vessels should not include straws, spoons, or
other
various drinking accessories. This is unfortunate, but because the vessel is a standalone item and cannot directly correlate to the drink that may eventually be inside it. Therefore, drinking accessories are inappropriate.
DESIGN <sketch> APPEARANCE <description>
- Sketch out the appearance
description
of your vessel.
Note: Max of 50 characters.
- The Appearance is the short description visible in
WARES
, in
INV
, and
seen with
IH
.
-
Commas
are not permitted in the Appearance of crafted items.
- This should begin with a lowercase
letter
and NOT end in a full stop.
- This must feature the name of the canvas type as a main noun.
- Examples are: 'a bone drinking horn' and 'a snifter of amber glass'.
- This should not be a full sentence.
- Do not include actions or
reactions
, simply what the item is.
- Do not include the drink this will eventually contain.
DESIGN <sketch> DROPPED <description>
- The dropped description is what is seen via
LOOK
when your item is
dropped in a location.
- This must be ONE complete sentence that begins with a capital letter
and ends with a full stop.
Note: Max of 80 characters.
-
Describe
just the item, not any drink, or possible player reactions or
actions.
- No superfluous
punctuation
(
question
marks
, exclamations, etc.)
- The main noun in the sentence must be the same as the compound type so
that people know what to call it when they want to interact with it.
-
Things
like "A wine glass stands upon a table here." is not acceptable. If
it were dropped on the highway there would be no table to stand on.
DESIGN <sketch> EXAMINED <description>
- This is what people see when an item is examined/probed.
- This should only describe the item, no subjective terms, reactions,
opinions,
emotions
, or responses.
- Do not describe a beverage inside the vessel.
The INVESSEL portion of a beverage's design will append to the Examined
of your vessel's description and will serve to describe any drink
currently within.
- Use at least two full sentences.
- Must begin with a capital letter and end with a full stop.
- This must feature the main noun of the item.
- Do not try to format your
descriptions
in the
editor
. When using the
editor put your entire description on line one. The editor will properly
format it for you.
- Do not "evoke" or
tell
the reader what
sense
/
emotion
/impression your
design gives. ALWAYS
SHOW
DO NOT TELL.
- Vessel crafters are not enchanters, magic is not permitted in crafted
designs.
- Designs should describe the item, not the onlooker, use of "you" and "your"
is prohibited.
- Single spacing only.
- Do not use words that require accents or other non-English punctuation,
these cannot be replicated in
Achaea
and will be treated as
misspellings
.
E.G: Applique would require an acute
accent
over the e.
- Feet, inches, yards, etc, no
metric
measurements.
- Please use a GB English spellcheck and the Oxford
comma
. Achaea does not
use American spelling.
- There are
children
in Achaea, keep your designs tasteful.
DESIGN <sketch> PRIVATE ON|OFF
- At a cost of 1000gp you can hide your designs from
others
when they do
NDS MATCH searches. This flag may be set on the sketch or after approval
with the corresponding NDS
command
.
DESIGN <sketch> HIDEMARK ON|OFF
- At the cost of 1000gp you can hide the crafters
mark
. This means that
when the crafted item is probed it will not show the line
saying
"It
bears the distinctive mark of "".
DESIGN <sketch> SUBMIT [URGENT]
- PLEASE PROOFREAD YOUR DESIGN BEFORE SUBMITTING - See
HELP PROOFREADING
for guidance
- Submit your sketch for approval by the
Crafting
Council.
- The cost to submit a design does scale based on the number of
times
it has had to be submitted,
PROOF
and check your work!
Urgent:
First submission 10000 gold, second submission 12000 gold, third submission 15000 gold, fourth submission 20000 gold, fifth submission (and cap) 30000 gold.
Standard: 1000 -> 2000 -> 5000 -> 10000 -> 20000
First submission 1000 gold, second submission 2000 gold, third submission 5000 gold, fourth submission 10000 gold, fifth submission (and cap) 20000 gold.
- Must submit from the Beverages cellar in Delos (unless you possess the Crest of the Crafting Council.)
- The addition of URGENT places a design in a high priority
queue
and costs an increased amount of gold.
- You can have up to ten designs in the submission queue. This is total, across all
tradeskills
.
- PLEASE PROOFREAD YOUR DESIGN BEFORE SUBMITTING - See below and
HELP PROOFREADING
for assistance!
DESIGN REQUEST <ID>
- Use this command at the Beverages cellar when you have been notified (by
message
) that your design has been approved.
- Have the gold required in your hands.
PROOFREADING
(see also HELP PROOFREADING)
=========================================
This section will be embellished in the future when common mistakes become
apparent.
ITEM TYPE
- Make sure that the compound type used matches the main noun in all aspects
of this design.
APPEARANCE
- Commas are not permitted in the Appearance of crafted items.
- This should begin with a lowercase letter and NOT end in a full stop.
- This must feature the name of the item as a main noun.
- This should not be a full sentence.
- Do not include actions or reactions, simply what the item is.
- Do not reference a drink.
DROPPED
- This must be ONE complete sentence that begins with a capital letter
and ends with a full stop.
- Describe just the item, not possible player reactions or actions.
- The main noun in the sentence must be the same as the canvas name so
that people know what to call it when they want to pick it up, without
having to type 'info here.'
- Do not reference a drink.
EXAMINED
- This should only describe the item, no subjective terms, reactions,
opinions, emotions, or responses. SHOW DON'T TELL
- Do not reference a drink.
- Use at least two complete sentences, NO FRAGMENTS.
- Do not "evoke".
- All numbers must be written out in words. "one, two, three".
- Feet, inches, yards, etc, no metric measurements.
- Please use a GB English spellcheck, Achaea does not use American spelling.
- Achaea uses the "Oxford comma" when using lists. This means that when
writing a list, a comma should be used before the "and". E.G: a red,
white, and blue scarf.
- Single spacing always and only. Check for double spaces especially after
full stops, and if you have copied text from another format.
- Designs should describe the item, not the onlooker, use of "you" and "your"
are prohibited.
If you do not have a Beverages
license
, see HELP BEVERAGES!
For a full and detailed listing of the entire NDS output, see
HELP DESIGN
(See also: HELP CRAFTING GUIDELINES)
