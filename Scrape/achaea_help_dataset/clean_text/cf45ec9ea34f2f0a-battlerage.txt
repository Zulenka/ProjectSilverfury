9.75 Battlerage
Battlerage
is the
name
for a series of
abilities
that everyone unlocks within the Attainment
skill
. (See
AB
ATTAINMENT)
While many of your
class
-related abilities in
Achaea
are only useful when used
on
other
adventurers, the Battlerage abilities are only useful when used on
denizens
.
Further, unlike most other
skills
, Battlerage is not learned by using
lessons
- you will gain new abilities by
levelling
up.
There are six
BR
abilities per class, as well as six for each colour of
Dragon
,
so there is a large gap in
levels
between the abilities.
Within Attainment, you will get Battlerage abilities at:
*
Level
6
* Level 10
* Level 20
* Level 35
* Level 50
* Level 65
* Level 100 - Provoke
Dragons
will have all of the abilities and those
who
are Elder Dragons will
find their Battlerage abilities switch when they switch Dragon colour.
WHAT IS
RAGE
?
-------------
All Battlerage abilities are powered by a
stat
called Rage.
Rage is gained by doing damage to denizens. Rage is gained at a constant rate
based upon your
attack
speed
. When you gain rage, you cannot gain rage again
for a short
time
immediately afterwards. If you attack a
denizen
with multiple
simultaneous
attacks
, you will gain the full amount of rage of your attack
speed based on the very first attack, and subsequent attacks will not generate
any rage.
When you gain Rage, everyone in your
group
also gains some portion of that
Rage. How much depends on how many people are in your group. Remember,
everybody in the group will get the below % of the Rage generated by every
other person in the group, providing they're in the same room.
2
people = 10%
3
people = 8%    4 people = 7%
5
people = 6%      6 people = 6%    7 people = 5%
8 people = 4%      9 people = 3%   10 people = 2%
11 people = 1.5%   12 people = 1%   13 people =.8%
14 people = .5%    15 people =.3%
HOW DO I SEE HOW MUCH RAGE I HAVE?
----------------------------------
By default, you will be notified every time you gain Rage. You can
config
this
off/on by doing CONFIG RAGEMSG GAIN to turn it on, and CONFIG RAGEMSG without
that option to turn it off.
CONFIG COLOUR RAGEMSG <colour> will let you pick a colour for the associated
Rage
messages
.
You can also
configure
your
prompt
to
show
you Rage by using R in a standard
prompt or *R in a
custom
prompt. (
HELP PROMPT
will explain more.) Please note
that if you're using the Achaea
web
client
and can't see your prompt, it's
probably because you've checked the option to turn off
prompts
in your settings
window
(accessible via the gears in the lower right of the client).
Finally, you can quickly see your current rage by doing SHOW RAGE, SHOW
BATTLERAGE, BATTLERAGE, BRAGE or simply BR.
USING RAGE
----------
Before you can use Rage, you need to have Rage. See above!
Each Battlerage
ability
(AB ATTAINMENT <ability name>) will
tell
you how much
Rage it costs and how long the cooldown is for it. There are also
configuration
options to CONFIG RAGEMSG that will allow you to see when an ability cooldown
ends.
There are, roughly, four types of Battlerage abilities:
* Damage (which may be straight damage, damage-over-time, or some
combination
thereof).
* Afflicting (Afflicting a denizen with
something
, like weakness or amnesia.)
* Conditional Damage (Extra damage if the denizen has a particular
affliction.)
*
Shield
Breakers (
Remove
a denizen's shield)
Rage must be gained continually in order to be kept. After 15
seconds
of not
using or gaining Rage, it will quickly fade.
RAGE-BASED
AFFLICTIONS
----------------------
The
following
afflictions can be inflicted on denizens via Battlerage:
* Sensitivity  - The denizen takes 33% extra damage for 8 seconds.
* Amnesia      - The denizen misses the next 2-3 attacks.
* Recklessness - The denizen can't shield for 15 seconds.
* Weakness     - The denizen does 66% of normal damage for 7 seconds.
* Feared       - The denizen flees every couple seconds for 8 seconds.
*
Aeon
- The denizen attacks at 66% normal speed for 6 seconds.
* Clumsy       - The denizen misses 33% of attacks for 7 seconds.
* Inhibit      - The denizen doesn't actively
heal
for 9 seconds.
*
Charm
- The denizen will attack other
mobs
that you have beef with
for 5 seconds.
* Stun         - The
mob
doesn't do
anything
for 4 seconds.
RAGE CONFIGURATION SETTINGS
---------------------------
There are three different categories of Battlerage notifications, each of which
can be enabled separately.
The three categories are:
GAIN
- Notifications about generating and
losing
rage.
ABCOOLDOWNS
- Notifications about the cooldown of a specific ability.
GLOBALCOOLDOWNS
- Notifications about the global 1-second cooldown on all Battlerage
abilities.
Use CONFIG RAGEMSG <options> to
control
these configuration settings.
Examples:
CONFIG RAGEMSG
- Turns off all three categories of notifications.
CONFIG RAGEMSG GAIN
- Turns gain notifications on, and all cooldown notifications off.
CONFIG RAGEMSG GAIN ABCOOLDOWNS
- Turns notifications about rage gain and specific ability cooldowns on,
but global cooldown notifications are off.
