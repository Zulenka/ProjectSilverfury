11.7.21 Haskor, the Patron of Heroes
From the
crucible
of conflict unending comes
Haskor
, the Self-Made, a testament to
Divinity
born in the blood and mayhem of a battlefield on His native
world
Khlenticthlisth. Drawn forth from the womb with the Horde in His blood, the first sounds His ears heard were the clash of swords and the cries of the slaying. Rising to first
adventure
, then
conquest
, then immortality, and then at last true
Ascension
by Trials overcome upon the Pantheon's
garden
of
Achaea
, His is the
dominion
once held by
Matsuhama
, God of
Combat
. In the culmination of centuries and works set to motion, one of Ayar-now-Sarapis' Realms of Creation now at last finds a new face for its Eternal Warrior.
Yet Haskor's
saga
was ne'er one to submit to that which had gone Before. His is an epic writ red and bold across the pages of brutal
history
, allowing no
other
to eclipse His hard-won acclaim as Creation's Hero. It is acclaim not as the mere
reincarnation
experienced by lesser entities, but as a true
successor
by blade and by tale. It is said that His deeds carry all the numbers of the Sky's many
stars
, a litany sung by
priests
and admirers alike across a thousand bloodied fields. In such legends Haskor rose, and in Triumph does He reign.
Woe
betide those
who
stood against Him as
enemy
, for they are one and all now but a footnote in the tale of the God who conquered His own Destiny.
Providence's mechanisms in myth are enormous and cold, their ever-
moving
wheels a ceaseless grinding of
Time
to render all that was, is, and may yet be to dust upon the fields of Memory. But within His bravado the Patron of Heroes is an embodiment of Fate's mastery. If you too would break Destiny to heel, Haskor rules there to be but one means to tip the scales, but one means to place your hand upon the great Fulcrum of Eternity and make of it your own saga. Only blood,
running
red with toil and sweat to oil the cogs of Creation with endless glory.
The march of history across Achaea is one paved in
corpses
and woe. But the victor, the Hero, knows that we live in an epoch of
challenge
for which there is but one
key
to avert one's own desolation. It is neither oath nor
loyalty
, for Haskor cares little for the trivialities of oft-broken mortal promise. Among those He
champions
, it is but one
thing
: the sword.
