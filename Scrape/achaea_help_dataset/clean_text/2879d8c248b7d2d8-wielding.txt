13.4.1 WEARING AND WIELDING
For one-handed swords, you still have the old-fashioned
scabbards
. While for knives, dirks, and daggers, you now have sheaths. For one-handed axes, and maces you have
beltloops
and for those
who
really prefer brute force, for the two-handed
weapons
, there is the sturdy
baldric
. For those of you with a
thing
for whips and blackjacks, there are belthooks to hang them from.
All of these
weapon
holders must be worn in order to
draw
or
sheathe
a weapon.
The penultimate
command
for weapon and
shield
control
:
WIELD
[item1|NONE] [item2|NONE]
However, the alternatives for old school folks are listed below.
Putting a sword into a
scabbard
:
SHEATHE
SHEATHE [LEFT/RIGHT]
SHEATHE <weapon>
SHEATHE <holder>
Getting a sword out of a scabbard:
DRAW
DRAW [SALUTE/STRONG] [LEFT/RIGHT]
DRAW <weapon> [SALUTE/STRONG] [LEFT/RIGHT]
DRAW <holder> [SALUTE/STRONG] [LEFT/RIGHT]
Lashing a shield to a
backstrap
LASH <shield> TO <backstrap>
UNLASH LEFT|RIGHT
UNLASH <shield> [LEFT|RIGHT]
UNLASH <holder> [LEFT|RIGHT]
To
equip
(wield, ready, or
hold
)
something
in your hand(s), WIELD it.
WIELD [LEFT|RIGHT] <item>
For example, WIELD SWORD, or WIELD RIGHT RAPIER.
Additional
commands
related to wield:
WIELDED
-
shows
what you are
wielding
.
UNWIELD
<item|LEFT|RIGHT>  - unwield something.
SWAP HANDS                 - Swap wielded items to
other
hand.
To equip (aka wear) something on your body, arms, etc, WEAR it.
WEAR <item>     - put something on.
REMOVE
<item>   - take something off.
(See also:
HELP
WEAPONS)
