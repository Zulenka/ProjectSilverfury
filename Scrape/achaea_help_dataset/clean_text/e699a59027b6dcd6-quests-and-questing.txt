14.1 Quests and Questing
(See HELP NEWBIE QUESTS,
HELP
QUEST
LOG
)
In
Achaea
we have many types of
quests
, nearly all of which involve
denizens
(a.k.a.
mobs
or
NPCs
).
Quests may be simple (locating and delivering an item) or quite complex (with
many steps, puzzles, etc., that
might
take
days
or weeks to complete). They are
numerous and may be found all over the
world
.
The most basic quests, such as those found in adventurer
cities
and
newbie
areas, are simple to discover and relatively easy to complete. Simply GREET the
denizens you meet, or
say
something
such as "have any quests for
me
?" and you
will often be offered a quest. When you accept (by answering in the
affirmative, whether a spoken word or an
emote
), it will be added to your quest
log (see HELP QUEST LOG). Do as you have been instructed, and you will gain a
reward in the form of
experience
and/or
gold
!
As you progress in Achaea, quests may become more difficult to discover. You
will need to
look
for clues in what denizens have to say, and even carry on
conversations with them before they will offer you a quest. Many denizens have
multiple
things
to say, so
greeting
them multiple
times
may be useful.
Other
clues might be found in
journals
or
books
or other writings that you can
READ
. Read
everything
carefully: the appearance of each room you enter, the
descriptions
of the denizens you meet, and the written texts in books or
journals you may happen across in your
adventures
.
If part of a quest involves giving an item to an
denizen
, you have but to give
the item to it, and the denizen will then respond in a manner it deems
appropriate. Occasionally, of course, this may not be quite the manner you
hoped for, but this might simply be the result of the denizen being a bit
grouchy today.
Limitations on Quests
---------------------
Quest information can be shared freely between individuals provided that they do so via word of mouth. It is not permitted to compile volumes of quest information and solutions into help files, journals,
news
posts
, or other "compendiums".
(See HELP NEWBIE QUESTS, HELP QUEST LOG)
