7.31 Gear and Loot
Whilst
hunting
in
Achaea
you may find yourself coming into possession of some
gear
: specialist
equipment
obtained from and designed for hunting and
exploration
. In many ways similar to
artefacts
, gear
pieces
each
hold
distinct effects which provide various bonuses and augmentations while hunting
enemies
or while travelling about the land in search of
quests
and
other
adventure
.
Note: Interacting with gear is done via the GEAR
command
. These are not items in the traditional
sense
and thus do not bloat your
inventory
. They are entirely distinct.  While
equipped
, however, your specialist gear will be revealed descriptively to those
who
LOOK
at you.
Acquiring Gear
--------------
Most commonly, gear can be obtained from
denizens
in the
world
when slain. Almost every area in Achaea that is worth hunting has the chance for its denizens to relinquish a
piece
of
loot
when slain. Other sources of gear include completing the toughest quests and defeating the most difficult denizens, whose rare trinkets and keepsakes contain among some of the most valuable and powerful effects.
Some of these items may be distinctly flavoured for the area where they are obtained (
Annwyn
or the
Underworld
, for example), but many are simply the spoils of adventuring now left in your hands to enjoy.
GEAR LIST <filters> (filters being ALL, EQUIPPED, UNEQUIPPED, or a slot)
- Displays your acquired gear based on your specific parameters.
Gear Slots
----------
Gear pieces all adhere to a specific "slot" in which they can be worn. Typically these include the head, hands, feet, chest, and trinket slots. Only one item can be equipped in each slot.
GEAR
EQUIP
<gear|set>
GEAR
UNEQUIP
<gear|set>
-
Equips
or unequips a piece of gear, or a set
Gear Effects and Sets
----------
All gear items carry their own independent effects and bonuses. These range in magnitude based on rarity, the set (if any), and an element of randomness in addition. The types of effects on items vary wildly, ranging from additional damage on your
attacks
to specific resistances, granular bonuses against certain
denizen
types, and a plethora of more distinct powers, such as that akin to the Rageblade
relic
of old...
GEAR
PROBE
<gear>
- Examine a piece of gear to
learn
its slot, its rarity, its set (if any), remaining
decay
time
, any constraints on where or how it operates (for instance a certain
environment
, area, or amount of
battlerage
), and of course its effect.
GEAR SHOWOFF <gear> [FULL]
-
Show
off the
description
of a piece of gear to all present.
- FULL will additionally reveal the effect to those present.
Some pieces combine to provide an extra benefit when worn as a full set. Sets typically consist of four pieces, with the trinket slot excluded. When probing a piece of gear, you will be able to determine which set, if any, it belongs to.
GEAR PREVIEW <set>
- While
holding
at least one piece from a set, view the bonus effect for that set, whether or not you currently have it equipped.
Rarity
------
Rarity determines both how long a gear item will last, and the potential magnitude of its effect when obtained. Gear items come in three possible tiers of rarity:
- Common gear has the shortest decay time and lower magnitude of effects (typically the lower end of the possible range).
- Rare gear has a longer decay time than common and higher magnitude of effects (typically the higher end of the possible range).
- Remnant gear evokes echoes of ancient civilisations and technologies long forgotten, thus it has the highest potential decay time and its magnitudes can exceed even the assigned maximum.
Scrap
-----
Surplus gear pieces can be scrapped to obtain arcane ephemera. This unique material can then be utilised in transformation (via
Augmentation
) or
restoration
(via
Armoursmithing
), either via
trade
(see
HELP
GEAR
TRADING
), or directly if you possess the
abilities
yourself.
GEAR SCRAP <piece#>
- Break down a piece of gear into arcane ephemera.
- The amount generated is based on the remaining decay time on the piece.
GEAR LIST ALL
- Show your full list of gear as well as any ephemera obtained.
- Ephemera cannot be accumulated beyond 999 units.
Restoring and Transforming Gear
-------------------------------
Armoursmiths and those skilled with Augmentation will be able to offer their services in a gear trade to restore and transform gear. This costs the owner an amount of arcane ephemera.
Restoration will restore your piece up to its maximum original decay time, while Transformation will
attempt
to
reroll
a piece's effect into another from those available within its set and slot.
In cases where the same affix is rolled as the original, the magnitude will still be generated fresh.
A piece of gear may only be adjusted (restored or transformed) up to three
times
, and both restoring and transforming count towards this limit.
For example: If I restore a piece twice, I can only restore it a third time, or transform it once before it will no longer be possible to adjust any further.
Note: trinkets cannot be transformed.
Gear Trading
--------------
Trading gear between adventurers uses a unique interface in order to facilitate the exchange of pieces. When engaging in a trade proposal, you will enter negotiation with the other
party
and be prompted on how to proceed. This does not follow typical Achaea item exchanges and gear does not behave like typical items.
HELP GEAR TRADING contains full details on exchanging gear and should be consulted next.
