12.5.19 The Chapel of All Gods
Designed by the
famous
architect Lantis DeGage as a tribute to the
Divine
during the early
days
of the
Seleucarian
Empire, the
Chapel
of All
Gods
was
destroyed during the
War
of the Deeps, when it was used as a refuge for
townsfolk from the nearby town of
Thera
.
Lost
for generations afterwards,
the chapel's ruins were eventually rediscovered in 455 AF by a small
group
led by the archaeologist Duris. Since then, it has been restored to its
former glory after years of painstaking excavation and now stands once more
as a monument to the wonder of the Divine and a memorial to the lives that
were lost there.
