7.32 Gear Trading
Trading
gear
between adventurers uses a unique interface in order to facilitate the exchange of
pieces
. When engaging in a
trade
proposal, you will enter negotiation with the
other
party
and be prompted on how to proceed.
This does not follow typical
Achaea
item exchanges as gear does not behave like typical items.
Interacting with Trade Negotiations:
GEAR TRADE BEGIN <trader>
- Initiate a trade request with someone.
GEAR TRADE RESUME <trader>
- Resume a paused trade.
GEAR TRADE LIST
- Inspect any incomplete trade negotiations.
GEAR TRADE REVIEW <number|name>
- Review the details of a pending trade.
GEAR TRADE ABORT <number|name>
- Abort a pending trade.
Specific trading
Commands
:
ADD <amount> <
GOLD
|CREDITS>
- Add gold or
credits
to the trade.
ADD <gear1> [gear2] [gear...n]
- Add gear to the trade.
OFFER <TRANSFORMATION|RESTORATION>
- If you are able to transform or restore gear (via
augmentation
and
armoursmithing
respectively), offer your services.
HELP
- View trading commands
PROBE
<gear ID>
- See details about gear being offered.
REMOVE
<amount> <GOLD|CREDITS>
- Remove gold or credits from the trade.
REMOVE <gear1> [gear2] [gear..n]
- Remove gear from the trade.
RESTART
- Restart your proposal.
REVIEW
- See the current trade proposal.
SEAL
- Seal your trade offer.
QUIT
-
Leave
this trade for now.
CONCLUDE
- Conclude your trade and request confirmation from your partner.
A Note on
Restoration
and Transformation
----------------------------------------
Armoursmiths and those skilled with Augmentation will be able to offer their services in a gear trade to restore and transform gear. This costs the owner an amount of arcane ephemera (obtained via gear scrapping, see
HELP
GEAR).
Restoration will restore your
piece
up to its maximum original
decay
time
, while Transformation will
attempt
to
reroll
a piece's effect into another from those available within its set and slot.
In cases where the same affix is rolled as the original, the magnitude will still be generated fresh.
A piece of gear may only be adjusted (restored or transformed) up to three
times
, and both restoring and transforming count towards this limit.
For example: If I restore a piece twice, I can only restore it a third time, or transform it once before it will no longer be possible to adjust any further.
Note: trinkets cannot be transformed.
