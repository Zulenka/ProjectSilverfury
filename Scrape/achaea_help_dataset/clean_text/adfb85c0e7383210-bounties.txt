6.38.1 City Bounties
The
bounty
system
provides a way for
cities
to track and reward retribution for offences against the
city
.
VIEWING
BOUNTIES
----------------
To see current bounties,
go
to your city's bounty
board
and use the
syntax
:
CITY BOUNTY LIST
CITY BOUNTY INFO <bounty #>
These can be found in the
following
locations:
Ashtan
- Northern Parade of Zarathustra.
Cyrene
- Steps before the courthouse of Cyrene.
Eleusis
- Above the fields near a bridge.
Hashan
- A dark hallway.
Mhaldor
- Before the gates of the Baelgrim Fortress.
Targossas
- Scythia Square.
CLAIMING
A BOUNTY
-----------------
Claiming a bounty is open to all members of the city's
army
. To undertake a bounty, go to the bounty board and use the syntax:
CITY BOUNTY
CLAIM
<bounty #>
Doing so informs the
target
that he or she has a bounty on his or her head. Similarly, this opens up the claimant to
attack
from the target whilst the bounty remains in effect.
When the target is killed, you will immediately receive your reward.
ADDING/CANCELLING BOUNTIES
--------------------------
Defence
ministers
may add bounties to the board, or cancel them, with the following syntax:
CITY BOUNTY ADD <person> <reason> <reward amt> [
GOLD
|
CREDITS
]
CITY BOUNTY CANCEL <bounty #>
Gold rewards are deducted from the Defence Ministry coffers, while the credits are withdrawn from a dedicated pool.
This pool is replenished annually, and is configurable by the city leader. They may specify the size of the pool with:
CITY BOUNTY CREDITS AVAILABLE <amount>
Some notes about adding and cancelling bounties:
- These are for organisational
crimes
only.*
- There can be only one unclaimed bounty on a person's head at a
time
.
- The maximum bounty reward is 50,000 gold, or 100 credits.
- All bounties require a reason between 10 and 50 characters.
- Bounties display on the city
logs
when added and on the millog when claimed or completed.
- Bounties last
6
Achaean
months
from the time they are claimed.
-
Failed
bounties may not be re-filed.
- Bounties that are close to expiration may not be cancelled and re-filed.
- You may not get through a bounty then go down the next crime "on the list" and file another. Once a bounty is completed or failed, you must
consider
ALL previous bountiable crimes as cleared from that person's record. You may not file another bounty on them unless they commit a new crime after that.
- Bounty filers are responsible for the bounties filed. Do your homework.
* For the purposes of bounty filing, vivify and exterminate are considered organisational crimes.
Conquest
is not unless it is
attacking
a mutually allied area.
* In addition, to keep
things
simple, if someone
attacks
one of your
citizens
inside city limits, you may EITHER file a bounty or that
citizen
may file a private
contract
. If someone (or their
group
, do not try to find a loophole) kills more than one person in what could reasonably be considered one extended
engagement
, then you may bounty regardless of
contracts
filed.
