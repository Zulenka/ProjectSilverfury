12.8.13 Ghezavat
Coiled beneath the shifting sands of the
Mhojave
Desert
, the commune of
Ghezavat
thrived for centuries without mortal detection until the year 849 AF. Built as a
sanctuary for the ill and the ancient, all
who
live there suffer from a form of
mortality sickness and
decay
that has exiled them from the surface.
Bound
in tenuous peace by the mysterious Concords, the
culture
of secrecy
pervading the recently-exposed commune is as essential to
Ghezavati
life as the
fierce dichotomy betwixt those who court the Sun and those who revere only
Death
.
Long-dead
Gods
and royalty are still worshipped under the dunes, and the
relics
of the past are as cherished as the present.
