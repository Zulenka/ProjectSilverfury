15.15 OOC Combat Communication
While we accept that a certain
level
of
automation
in
combat
situations is
inevitable, there are two clear cut situations where we
draw
a line. The
following
actions are not permitted under any circumstances, and will incur
administrative action similar to
other
automated activity as outlined in
HELP
AUTOMATION:
- Controlling other
clients
via issued
commands
. Do not play the game for
other people, basically. This is considered
character
sharing
and may result
in the loss of all characters involved.
Auto
targetting
is permitted here.
Automating
the actions of another character is not. Examples include
triggered blocking on
command
, using line of sight
attacks
, beckoning, or
any other triggered response which can be ordered by someone else to force
others
to carry out a game action. This is considered playing for someone
else and is not permitted.
- Using out of character/out of game
channels
to pass large amounts of
relevant real
time
information. This is more a common
sense
one.
Talking
to your buddies on
Discord
about stuff is fine. Passing large amounts of
game state or real time data isn't. Don't metagame, don't use
OOC
means to
circumvent game mechanics.
- If you are sending so much information that you require it to be gagged
by others, to the
point
where anyone without your gags is getting unbearably
spammed: this is no longer
communicating
with a character but with a
system
and
falls
under unacceptable
levels
of automated information relaying.
-
Clans
built for the purposes of
mass
information reporting are not allowed.
See also
HELP TRIGGERS
,
HELP CHARACTERSHARING
, and
HELP AUTOMATION
