9.37.10 Beverages
HELP BEVERAGES
See also HELP BEVERAGE DESIGN, HELP VESSEL DESIGN, and
HELP CRAFTING GUIDELINES
The
Beverages
tradeskill
allows for the
design
, creation, and storage of a
vast variety of drinks and victuals. In many ways a counterpart to
cooking
,
the domain of the beverage maker is varied and diverse - ranging from simple
juices and
teas
to award-
winning
beers, perfectly distilled spirits, and even
vintage, cellar-
aged
wines.
Whether a consumer or a connoisseur, Beverages allows an unparalleled depth of
self
expression
in all
things
related to drinks. And you can even swig your
twenty year old cognac from a teacup if you so desire!
To become a professional beverage-maker, you must be trained by the craft
guild
of
Delos
.
You must be above
level
10 to access the beverages tradeskill.
BUY
BEVERAGES PERMIT
- Use this
command
at "A cool cellar" in Delos.
- This will cost 200
credits
.
- If you have a bronze
trade
seal, please
READNEWS
ANNOUNCE
4247
- Once purchased, this permit will
show
up on
SCORE
LICENSES
.
- This will never expire and cannot be traded in.
GAINING THE BEVERAGES
SKILL
- Make sure you have Bought your Beverages Permit as per above!
- Make sure you have a tradeskill slot empty
-
TRADESKILLS
will show your tradeskill slots
- SCORE LICENSES will show how many slots you have in full.
- Visit Amarisse, Secretary of the
Crafting
Council
- ASK AMARISSE GAIN BEVERAGES.
Once you are a licensed beverage maker, you will have basic
ability
in the
Beverages tradeskill.
There are four
specialisations
within the Beverages
profession
:
Each of these specialisations requires a
point
invested in it in order to
design and create related drinks, 1 point is given at inept, another at
fabled, and a third at transcendent.
SPECIALISE IN <spec>   - Spend a point in one of the specialisations.
UNSPECIALISE BEVERAGES - Will cost 100
lessons
and reset your specialisations.
Fermentation
-------------
Fermentation is the domain of the wine and beer specialist, allowing the
creation and design of various ales, porters, wines, and the like.
Someone specialised in fermentation can submit a design for a fermented drink,
FERMENT the drink once approved, place the fermented drink in a cellar to age,
and then
fill
the finished drink into
drinking
vessels to be
drunk
!
(See
HELP
BEVERAGES DESIGN)
Distillation
-------------
Distillation concerns itself with the design and creation of various spirits,
including but not limited to vodka, gin, whiskey, rum, and so on.
Someone specialised in distillation can submit a design for a distilled drink,
DISTILL the drink once approved, place the distilled drink in a cellar to age,
and then fill the finished drink into drinking vessels to be drunk!
(See HELP BEVERAGES DESIGN)
Refreshments
-------------
Refreshments is the matter of designing and creating a variety of
immediately-drinkable beverages. Typically the domain of non-alcoholic
drinks, the refreshment specialist is however also able to mix cocktails as
well as steep teas and kawhes, and press juices and cordials.
Someone specialised in refreshments can submit a design for a mixed, steeped,
or pressed drink, MIX/STEEP/PRESS the drink once approved, and
pour
into
drinking vessels to be drunk!
(See HELP BEVERAGES DESIGN)
Vessels
--------
To specialise in vessels is to pride oneself not on the drink itself but the
container
in which it resides. Often, an elaborate vessel can mask a bland and
pedestrian drink, and the opposite is also true! Those taking up the Vessels
craft shall find themselves able to design a variety of drinking
containers
,
ranging from traditional wine glasses and sophisticated snifters to a simple
mug
or a drinking horn.
These vessels can then be used to serve fermented, distilled, mixed, pressed,
or steeped drinks!
Anyone with access to a vessel
NDS
ID# can request vessels be made by one of
the
following
denizens
.
Vessels only
describe
the actual vessel, not the beverage it may eventually
contain. There is an aspect in beverage design (INVESSEL) that will add a
description
of the beverage itself when it is in the vessel.
Glassworkers, including Jessie in New
Thera
, make glass-based vessels.
Potters, including Gazban in El'
Jazira
, make clay-based vessels.
Stonemasons, including Palusta in
Xhaiden
Dale, make stone-based vessels.
Woodcarvers, including Tobe in Caer Witrin, make
wood
and
bone
-based vessels.
Blacksmiths, including Rico in New Thera, make metal-based vessels.
Leatherworkers, including Vanac in
Bitterfork
, make
leather
-based vessels.
All non-
barrel
/cask/keg
equipment
can be bought at "a cool cellar" in Delos.
(See HELP VESSEL DESIGN)
