Runewarden kill-path changes around Pithakhan +
Kena in Achaea
Scope and what counts as a “reference”
Your chat log describes a modern Runewarden approach that revolves around (a) forcing the opponent
below a mana threshold, (b) targeting head damage/breaks to make Pithakhan reliably drain mana, and (c)
leveraging Kena to deliver impatience to support a sustained lock (“true lock”). The most directly citable,
authoritative “this changed” references for that are Achaea’s official classleads patch posts, backed by the
(often more detailed, but sometimes older) AchaeaWiki ability-file transcriptions for Runelore.
For clarity: “Runewarden” here refers to the Achaea class that practices Weaponmastery + Runelore +
Discipline.
The recent mechanical changes that shifted Runewarden finishing
lines
Kena’s “fires” threshold moved to a much easier range
In the December 9, 2025 official classleads batch, Achaea changed KENA so that it “fires” when the target’s
mana is below 40%, instead of below 20%.
This is a major practical shift because 40% mana is dramatically easier to reach and maintain than 20% in
real PvP pacing (especially when paired with other mana pressure), which matches the conversation you
posted (“get someone below 40% mana”).
Pithakhan’s mana drain vs broken head was increased
That same December 9, 2025 classleads post also buffed PITHAKHAN: its mana drain “against targets with
a broken head” was increased from 10% to 13%.
The important part for your “references” request is that the patch note explicitly ties the drain increase to
broken head, which is why modern Runewarden kill-plans emphasize head damage/break windows.
Earlier enabling change: Pithakhan became reliable on damaged head
While not “recent” relative to March 2026, the key enabling rule that makes “head break guarantees Pith will
proc” believable is from the July 3, 2022 Runelore rework classleads: “PITHAKHAN now always fires when
striking a target with a DAMAGED HEAD.”

What Kena and Pithakhan do in Runelore, including the runeblade
“configuration” model
Runelore supports weapon-based rune effects through runeblades
Runelore explicitly supports sketching runes “on … weapons,” and many Runelore effects are designed to be
attached to a Runewarden’s runeblade (an empowered weapon).
AchaeaWiki’s Runelore page (which paraphrases/structures the in-game ability files) is especially useful
because it records both the “ground/totem” behavior and the “in configuration” behavior for each rune.
Kena: ground rune fear, but runeblade configuration delivers impatience
On the Runelore ability entry for Kena, the wiki describes two relevant layers:
As a rune on ground/totem, Kena “inspires fear” in an enemy who enters and sees it.
In configuration (i.e., runeblade configuration), the ability file lists the attunement condition and
the empower effect: historically recorded as “runeblade strikes a target with 20% mana or less” and
the empower effect “delivers impatience.”
Your “recent change” reference is that the official classleads patch updates that mana threshold to 40%
(replacing the older 20% number shown on many static references).
Why “impatience” matters to locks
Impatience is an affliction that “prevents you from focusing” and is cured by goldenseal.
The AchaeaWiki curing table likewise lists impatience as cured by goldenseal (or its mineral equivalent,
plumbum).
This is the critical bridge from “Kena fires at <40% mana” to “we can true lock”: Kena’s empower effect is
documented as delivering impatience, and impatience blocks focusing.
Pithakhan: weapon-based mana drain (runeblade-only), now pushed by head damage
rules
The Runelore ability entry for Pithakhan describes it as an “attack [on] mental reserves,” and—crucially for
your use—states that when sketched on a weapon it “enables the weapon to occasionally drain mana,” but
“this will only apply if the weapon is a runeblade.”
The “how does head focus matter?” portion isn’t in the generic rune description; it’s in the classleads deltas:
July 2022: Pithakhan “always fires” when striking a target with a damaged head.

How weapon “configuration” works and why dual-cutting
Runewardens care
The 2022 Runelore rework introduced (and the current ability file documents) CONFIGURATION, which is
the system that lets runewardens put secondary runes (like Kena) into a runeblade setup that can become
“attuned” and then “empowered.” The ability file summary includes:
You can “sketch runic configurations upon your runeblade,” but only certain runes can be in
configuration, and they must be arranged about one of the core runeblade runes (explicitly
including Pithakhan).
Empowered runes “fire their effects after a successful weaponmastery attack resolves” (and for
multi-hit attacks like doubleslash, after the entire attack completes).
Configuration conditions are checked “only on the first hit” of multi-hit attacks, and dual-cutting or
dual-blunt runewardens are advised to wield their configured runeblade in the left hand.
This matters for your workflow because your chat log talks about running dual scimitars and using DSL
(doubleslash). In Weaponmastery, DSL is the core dual-cutting attack (the “Duality” ability) and it can accept
optional limb/venom parameters.
Putting those together yields a rules-based, citable explanation of why modern practice is “run Pith +
Kena” (i.e., Pithakhan as a core runeblade rune with Kena in configuration) and why the configured blade is
typically the left-hand one for dual cutting.
Why the meta shifted toward “pith + kena into head focus,” plus
where kelp stacking fits
The lock logic reflected in your chat now matches the official numbers
The December 2025 classleads update moved Kena’s trigger point to <40% mana and buffed Pithakhan’s
drain in the “broken head” state.
Separately, Pithakhan was already made consistent against a damaged head in the 2022 Runelore rework.
Taken together, it becomes much more realistic for Runewardens to keep an opponent in (or repeatedly
push them back into) the Kena-eligible mana band, which then repeatedly enables Kena’s impatience
delivery—supporting the “effectively you can true lock someone” framing from your quoted conversation.

“Build a kelp stack” has a concrete meaning in Achaea’s curing tables
Your chat says: “build a kelp stack meaning hit with venoms that need kelp to cure.” That maps directly onto
Achaea’s herb cure model as documented on AchaeaWiki’s Curing table: multiple afflictions share a single
herb cure, including a large “Kelp / Aurum” bucket.
The curing table lists several afflictions cured by kelp (or aurum), including asthma, clumsiness,
healthleech, hypochondria, sensitivity, and weariness.
Several of these are explicitly tied to common venoms:
Kalmia afflicts asthma and is cured by kelp.
Vernalius is a venom associated with weariness and is cured by kelp.
Prefarar is cited as a cause of sensitivity, and sensitivity is cured by kelp.
Xentio is cited as a cause of clumsiness, and clumsiness is cured by kelp.
So, “kelp stack” is a shorthand for forcing repeated kelp (or aurum) consumption and competing cures
inside that same herb channel—an approach consistent with how Achaea’s affliction curing is described (a
specific action/herb per affliction, with a cadence for herb use).
Weaponmastery envenoming is the delivery mechanism Runewardens rely on
In Weaponmastery, ENVENOM lets you “layer venoms on an edged weapon,” and each hit consumes one
venom in last-on/first-off order.
Since dual-cutting Runewardens deliver multi-hit sequences via DSL and can optionally specify venoms, the
“apply kelp-cure venoms before executing” portion of your chat is consistent with documented
Weaponmastery mechanics.
